The notion that we can assimilate highly advanced alien technology in the time it takes to change scenes is one of the most annoying and unrealistic parts of Star Trek, conjured up by writers looking to get out of corners they've written themselves into, with no real world knowledge of engineering. That's not to say that 1940s Earth wouldn't get some benefit from Quark's ship; of course they would. It just wouldn't be a revolutionary sea change in society. It would take decades to begin to understand even the most basic of technology on his ship, requiring our best minds; you're talking about a period in time when the LED was still just a theory, not a consumer product. Of far more value would be the knowledge contained inside Quark, Rom, and Nog's heads. Remember Data's quote from "Déjà Q", with my emphasis, "He has provided important theoretical guidance for Geordi's analysis of the Brel satellite Captain." Q is far more advanced than the Enterprise-D crew, as Quark and Co. are here, but he can't give Geordi a step-by-step procedure to modify the Enterprise's systems to solve their current dilemma. At best, he can point us in the right direction, by encouraging us to consider theories that hadn't even occurred to us, theories that are as mundane to him as simple addition is to us.

Imagine even a single replicator in our world today. How fast would we replicate replicators? How fast would we solve world hunger if we had a few replicators? A single replicator and some 24th century batteries powerful enough to run it could completely change our entire world in a very short time. The entire world's economy is based on how fast we can produce goods and raw resources. Now figure that we can suddenly replicate raw resources and completed goods, much quicker than any other production method. Wars would be fought over a machine that is essentially a wish granting machine. This was the big thing about Voyager in the delta quadrant, most of the species they encountered didnt have replicators. The Kazon specifically really wanted that technology, which was center conflict of Ceska's betrayal arc. Quark could also offer holodecks, transporters, or warp technology. Even the blueprints to these devices would push any primitive species far into the future very quickly.
